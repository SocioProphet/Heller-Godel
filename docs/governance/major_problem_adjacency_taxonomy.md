# Major-Problem Adjacency Taxonomy

Status: repo-grade governance artifact.  
Classification (Hodge Program lane): `hodge-method`. Adjacency taxonomy for the Heller-Godel apparatus; not Hodge evidence.  
Provenance: consolidated from Drive-side `HG_MPR_001_v0_2_revised_ranking.md` into repo-grade governance form.

## 1. Purpose

Several major mathematical problems are adjacent to the Heller-Godel apparatus along different axes. Without a taxonomy, “adjacent” becomes ambiguous and can drift into claims that Heller-Godel work bears on other major problems when the actual adjacency is methodological rather than substantive.

This taxonomy separates three forms of adjacency and classifies major problems accordingly.

## 2. Three axes of adjacency

| Axis | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| object-adjacent | Same mathematical objects, structures, or specific machinery |
| methodology-adjacent | Same problem shape or claim-discipline; different objects |
| outcome-adjacent | Similar phenomenon, such as finite order, comparison, or regulator output, but not the same problem |

A problem can appear on multiple axes. Methodology-adjacent or outcome-adjacent does not mean object-adjacent.

## 4. Implications of the taxonomy

Object-adjacent work is narrowly bounded. The Hodge conjecture is the only major-problem target with direct object adjacency, and even there the current Heller-Godel artifacts supply only Hodge-shaped method objects, not Hodge classes or algebraic cycles.

Methodology-adjacent work is not evidence. Yang-Mills, BSD, RH, abc, Mumford-Tate, and Temporal Mechanics may share claim-boundary or proof-fabric discipline, but Heller-Godel artifacts do not bear on their theorem state.

Outcome-adjacent work is diagnostic. Similar regulator or comparison phenomena may identify useful sanity checks or false-positive risks, but they do not promote the apparatus toward those problems.

## 5. What this taxonomy prevents

This taxonomy blocks drift patterns such as:

1. local regulator output becoming Beilinson-conjecture evidence;
2. finite monodromy becoming Tate or K3 data;
3. Heller-Godel comparison diagrams becoming motivic theorems;
4. YM methodology transfer becoming Yang-Mills theorem evidence;
5. architectural speculation becoming proof-bearing geometry.

## 6. What this taxonomy is and is not

### What it is

1. A controlled vocabulary for “adjacent.”
2. A governance artifact for scope discipline.
3. A way to distinguish substantive object overlap from methodological analogy.

### What it is not

1. Not a ranking of problems by importance.
2. Not a claim that any methodology-adjacent problem is approachable through Heller-Godel.
3. Not a research roadmap.
4. Not exhaustive.
