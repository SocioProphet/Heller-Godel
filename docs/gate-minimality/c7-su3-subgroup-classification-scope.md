## Purpose

C-7 is the closed connected subgroup classification obligation for the adopted A2 convention:

```text
path beta + SU(3) + central Z/3 + cubic invariant
```

The obligation is not to re-derive the classification theorem. The obligation is to fix which closed connected subgroups of SU(3) must be considered, then determine which are admissible under the A2 gate-minimality criteria.

Use Borel--de Siebenthal and standard compact Lie subgroup classification references as the classification background.

## Working class: closed connected reductive subgroups

C-7 works with closed connected reductive subgroups of SU(3), not parabolic subgroups of the complexified group.

The distinction matters:

- closed connected compact/reductive subgroup analysis is the relevant SU(3) gate-minimality input;
- parabolic subgroup language belongs to the complexified algebraic group setting and is not the object being classified here;
- the A2 gate criteria are representation/admissibility filters inside compact SU(3).

A later C-7 closure note may cite complexified language for comparison, but the working C-7 object is compact closed connected reductive subgroup data.

## A2 admissibility filters

A subgroup candidate must be checked against at least these filters:

1. Closed and connected subgroup of SU(3), within the compact reductive working class.
2. Compatibility with the central Z/3 datum generated by `omega I_3`, where `omega^3 = 1` and `omega != 1`.
3. Compatibility with the active-sector cubic invariant selected by C-8.
4. Compatibility with the spatial faithfulness condition from the A2 structural sketch.
5. No collapse back to a path-alpha Spin(3) encoding unless explicitly marked as the C-9 alternative.

C-8 is formulated in the paired Heller-Godel C-8 document but not closed as a theorem obligation. Any verdict depending on the cubic-invariant condition remains preliminary and must explicitly name C-8 as the dependency.

## Embedding disambiguation

The standard block `SU(2)` and the principal SU(2)-type embedding are not the same subgroup type.

The standard block embedding is the upper-left `2 x 2` subgroup of SU(3). It preserves a chosen `C^2 + C` branching and misses the scalar Z/3 center.

The principal SU(2)-type embedding is the irreducible 3-dimensional representation. Its image is naturally read as an SO(3) subgroup of SU(3). Calling this a principal SU(2) can obscure the fact that the center of SU(2) acts trivially in the spin-1 representation, so the image does not contain the SU(3) center.

The `U(2)` candidate is the standard `S(U(2) x U(1))` subgroup. It contains the relevant scalar center and is the principal proper connected candidate whose final admissibility depends on C-8.

## Center containment test

The A2 path-beta convention uses the scalar center:

```text
Z(SU(3)) = {I_3, omega I_3, omega^2 I_3}
```

A subgroup that misses `omega I_3` is strongly suspected inadmissible under any A2 gate criterion that load-bears on the central Z/3 datum. In this scope note, such candidates are marked inadmissible unless a later C-7 closure proof states a narrower criterion.

Subgroups containing the center are not automatically admissible. They must still pass C-8.

## C-8 dependency

C-8 is the cubic-invariant analog of A1 condition (v).

Every open verdict in this document depends on C-8 in the following concrete sense:

```text
Does the subgroup preserve the selected A2 cubic datum in the active-sector realization required by condition (v)?
```

For `U(2) = S(U(2) x U(1))`, center containment alone is insufficient. C-8 must decide whether the selected cubic invariant eliminates or admits this subgroup.

For full `SU(3)`, C-8 must verify the adopted convention's active cubic datum is the correct invariant to preserve.

## Preliminary eliminations

The trivial subgroup and torus candidates are preliminary eliminations because they are too small or abelian and cannot carry the full A2 gate object.

The standard block `SU(2)` candidate is a preliminary elimination because it does not contain the central `Z/3` datum as scalar `omega I_3`.

The principal irreducible `SO(3)` candidate is a preliminary elimination because it does not carry the SU(3) center. It remains useful only as a comparison surface to avoid path-alpha leakage.

`U(2)` / `S(U(2) x U(1))` is the major proper connected candidate that remains open pending C-8.

Full `SU(3)` is the adopted convention's expected admissible candidate.
