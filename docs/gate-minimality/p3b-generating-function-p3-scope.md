## 1. Statement of obligation

P3.b — Canonical Statistic and Generating Function at `p=3`. Specify the formal characterization of the canonical statistic `sigma_C` extended to ternary applications and the proof-class generating function `C_3(x)` at `p=3`, consistent with `HG-FND-002`.

P3.b closure must verify:

1. `sigma_C` extended to the P3.a ternary grammar is a well-defined canonical statistic in the `HG-FND-002` sense;
2. `C_3(x)` is the proof-class / proof-family generating function of the grammar constructed in `HG-MTH-014`;
3. the analytic properties required by `HG-FND-002` hold for `C_3(x)` at the scope required for P3.b: formal-series interpretation, analyticity at the origin, and existence of a positive radius of convergence.

This document is the scope of P3.b, not its closure.

Let F be a fixed proof fragment. For this revision, the intended default is a simply typed lambda-calculus / implicational propositional fragment where normal inhabitants can be represented by alpha-classes of eta-long beta-normal de Bruijn lambda terms.

## 3. A1 paradigm at p=2

The A1 paradigm in the manuscript is:

```text
C(x)=1+xC(x)^2=(1 - sqrt(1-4x))/(2x)

C_A=(A -> A -> A) -> A -> A

T ::= x | b(T,T)

where x:A and b:A -> A -> A.
```

In `HG-FND-002` terms, the A1 construction works as follows:

1. fix a type-level grammar `C_A=(A -> A -> A) -> A -> A`;
2. take canonical normal forms generated by `T ::= x | b(T,T)`;
3. apply the fixed constructor statistic `sigma_C` to the alpha-classes of eta-long beta-normal de Bruijn terms;
4. form the enumerator `T_phi^sigma(x)=sum x^{sigma(s)}`;
5. obtain the Catalan algebraic calibration series `C(x)=1+xC(x)^2`.

P3.b closure must produce the analogous identification at `p=3`, replacing the binary constructor `b` by the ternary constructor `t` from `HG-MTH-014`.

## 4. Requirements on the p=3 generating function

P3.b closure must verify four requirements.

### S1 — Statistic well-definedness

The statistic `sigma_C` extended to count ternary applications must be well-defined on alpha-classes of eta-long beta-normal de Bruijn lambda terms of type:

```text
C_A^(3) = (A -> A -> A -> A) -> A -> A.
```

Specifically:

1. the statistic must respect alpha-equivalence;
2. the use of de Bruijn representatives must prevent variable-name artifacts;
3. the count must be finite for each normal form;
4. the ternary constructor changes term shape but does not change the statistic definition: lambda nodes, application nodes, and variable leaves remain the counted objects.

### S2 — Formal-series enumeration

`C_3(x)` as defined by the recursion:

```text
C_3(x)=1+x C_3(x)^3
```

must be the formal-series enumerator:

```text
C_3(x)=sum_{n >= 0} a_n x^n
```

where `a_n` is the number of alpha-classes of eta-long beta-normal terms of type `C_A^(3)` with statistic value `n`, under the constructor-shape convention selected by P3.a.

P3.b closure must verify:

```text
a_n = FC_n^(3)=1/(2n+1) * binom(3n,n).
```

### S3 — Analyticity at origin

`C_3(x)` must be analytic at `x=0` with:

```text
C_3(0)=1.
```

This is the formal-series condition lifted to a convergent series in a neighborhood of the origin. P3.b closure must show that a positive radius of convergence exists. The exact radius location and Puiseux exponent are traceability values in `HG-MTH-014` and are P3.c-grade obligations, not P3.b closure claims.

### S4 — Continuation discipline

Following `HG-FND-002` continuation discipline, P3.b closure must state how `C_3(x)` is continued or represented beyond the formal power series at the origin.

For A1, the manuscript records the explicit closed form:

```text
C(x)=(1 - sqrt(1-4x))/(2x).
```

For `p=3`, the closed form involves cubic radicals and is not required as the default closure route. P3.b closure may instead state `C_3(x)` implicitly via:

```text
C_3(x)=1+x C_3(x)^3
```

with the branch selected by the condition `C_3(0)=1`. This implicit algebraic specification is the candidate default representation for P3.b closure.

## 5. Candidate verification strategies

This section records candidate approaches for P3.b closure, not adopted choices.

### Strategy V1: Direct enumeration verification

For small `n`, enumerate alpha-classes of `C_A^(3)`-typed normal forms by hand or by checker and verify the count matches `FC_n^(3)`.

Strength: direct combinatorial verification.

Weakness: does not scale and does not verify the functional equation in general.

### Strategy V2: Recursive structural verification

Show that the term grammar:

```text
T ::= x | t(T,T,T)
```

directly gives the recursion:

```text
C_3(x)=1+x C_3(x)^3.
```

Any non-trivial term is `t(T1,T2,T3)` for three subterms, and the base case `x` contributes the constant term `1`.

Strength: structural correctness without exhaustive enumeration.

Weakness: requires care that the statistic `sigma_C` extends to the ternary case in the implied way, with a single constructor contribution plus subterm contributions under the chosen constructor-shape convention.

### Strategy V3: Type-theoretic equivalence via normal-form theorem

Cite a theorem-grade normal-form result that eta-long beta-normal terms of the relevant type expression are in bijection with rooted ternary trees of the selected shape, then apply Fuss-Catalan enumeration.

Strength: clean reduction to a known combinatorial result.

Weakness: requires the equivalence theorem to be cited at theorem-grade, or else this strategy inherits another candidate-grade dependency.

P3.b closure should select one or combine them and verify S1 through S4.
