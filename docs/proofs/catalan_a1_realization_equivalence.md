# Catalan A1 Realization Equivalence Vocabulary

Status: theorem-support note.  
Scope: Catalan A1 fixture only.  
Depends on: Appendix A, especially Theorem A.7 and Theorem A.8.

## Purpose

This note introduces vocabulary for discussing admissible replacements of the Catalan A1 realization data without changing the proof state of Paper I / D1.

The aim is narrow: make precise what it means for two Catalan A1 realization choices to be equivalent for the finite `mu_2` output. This supports Theorem A.8 and prepares any future moduli-style discussion, while preserving the existing claim boundary.

## Objects

A Catalan A1 realization pair is a pair

```text
(Gamma, e)
```

where:

1. `Gamma` is a selected Lyapunov loop representative in the `SO(3)` factor or stated two-fold analogue used by the Catalan A1 fixture;
2. `e` is the selected encoding homomorphism from the cyclic source generated by `Gamma` into the Klein-bottle group;
3. the finite output is evaluated by composing `e` with the pulled-back Klein-bottle `mu_2` holonomy.

The representative pinned by the v1 harness is:

```text
Gamma_Lyap(t) = R_z(2*pi*t)
e_*([Gamma_Lyap]) = r
Hol(r) = -1 in mu_2
```

## Admissibility

A realization pair `(Gamma, e)` is admissible when all of the following hold:

1. `Gamma` represents the nontrivial generator of `pi_1(SO(3)) ~= Z/2` in the fixture's selected `SO(3)` factor or two-fold analogue.
2. `e` sends the selected Lyapunov generator to the Klein-bottle orientation-reversing generator `r`.
3. The associated Floquet phase evaluates to `-1 in mu_2` on the selected generator.

These are the same conditions used in Theorem A.8. They are fixture conditions, not a general encoding theorem.

## Output map

Define the Catalan A1 finite-output map

```text
Omega_A1(Gamma, e) = Hol(e([Gamma])) in mu_2.
```

For admissible Catalan A1 realization pairs, Theorem A.8 states that

```text
Omega_A1(Gamma, e) = -1.
```

Thus `Omega_A1` is constant on the admissible Catalan A1 realization surface.

## Realization equivalence for the closed fixture

Two admissible realization pairs `(Gamma, e)` and `(Gamma_prime, e_prime)` are `mu_2-output equivalent` when

```text
Omega_A1(Gamma, e) = Omega_A1(Gamma_prime, e_prime).
```

By Theorem A.8, all admissible Catalan A1 realization pairs are `mu_2-output equivalent`.

A stricter relation may be useful later:

```text
(Gamma, e) ~_strong (Gamma_prime, e_prime)
```

when both representatives are connected through allowed loop deformations that preserve the selected nontrivial generator and both encoding maps agree on the selected generator. This stronger relation is not needed for Theorem A.8, and this note does not claim that it classifies all gate-level realizations.

## What this does not claim

This note does not claim:

1. uniqueness of the gate group;
2. uniqueness of the loop representative;
3. realization equivalence for non-admissible pairs;
4. an odd-prime analogue;
5. a moduli space of proof-class realizations;
6. a general sentence-to-gate compiler;
7. a Hodge-theoretic strengthening.

The only assertion carried forward is the finite-output statement already proved in Theorem A.8: admissible Catalan A1 realization pairs have the same `mu_2` output.

## Future use

A later proof-moduli front can refine this note by adding:

1. a typed registry of admissible realization data;
2. explicit morphism rules between realization records;
3. a proof that `Omega_A1` descends to the selected equivalence quotient;
4. a separate comparison between `mu_2-output equivalence` and stronger gate-level equivalence.

Those refinements are future work and are not required for the current D1 proof closure.
